# Distribution & growth playbook

Anas keeps circling the same core problem from different angles: if AI lets you ship product at lightning speed, distribution — not product — is the real bottleneck. The threads so far: (1) bootstrap tactic for founders with no audience and no budget — free-tool arbitrage to aggregate users, convert them later; (2) the paid, data-driven motion once something's live — reverse-engineer winning campaigns, scrape ad data, build ICPs off real conversion signals, track every dollar; (3) scrappy community-hijack plays — e.g. taking over dead subreddits as ready-made marketing surface. Together they sketch a rough go-to-market stack: find underpriced attention (free tools, abandoned communities), aggregate it, then run predictive sprints against it.
